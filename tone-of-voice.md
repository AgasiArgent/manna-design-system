# Manna Coffee — Tone of Voice Guide

**Date:** 2026-04-06
**Source:** Brand Foundation (docs/brand-foundation-draft.md), CEO interviews, design system
**Slogan:** Coffee, Love & Robots

---

## 1. Voice Principles

Manna's voice is the same everywhere. Tone shifts by audience and context — but the personality stays constant.

### The Voice in One Sentence

**Manna sounds like a successful friend who built something real — direct, warm, and backed by numbers.**

### Five Pillars

| Pillar | What It Means | Red Line |
|---|---|---|
| **Direct** | Lead with the number, the fact, the answer. No fluff before the point. | Never vague. Never "significant returns" when you can say "$800-1,200/month." |
| **Warm** | Human first, company second. We talk like people, not brands. | Never cold/corporate. Never "leverage," "synergize," "disrupting the space." |
| **Honest** | Say what's true, including the hard parts. Real margins, real timelines. | Never inflate. Company marketing says 212% ROI — we say 50-80%. Never hide risks. |
| **Confident** | We know our product. 25 years of business, 8,500 leads, real installations. | Never arrogant. "Our kiosks deliver barista quality" — not "we're the best." |
| **Empowering** | The partner is the hero. We give them tools, not dependence. | Never paternal. "Your business, your profit" — not "we'll take care of everything." |

## 3. Language Rules

### Always Use

| Term | Instead of | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Smart kiosk / coffee kiosk | Vending machine | "Vending machine" = instant coffee distrust |
| Coffee bar | Vending point | Elevates the product |
| Bean-to-cup | Freshly brewed / fresh | Specific, technical, trustworthy |
| Partner / licensee | Franchisee / customer | They're partners, not buyers |
| License | Franchise fee | We're not a franchise. Different model, different connotation. |
| Plug & play | Easy to install | Concrete image vs vague claim |
| Barista-quality | Premium / gourmet | Benchmarks against something real |
| Smart / automated | Unmanned / self-service | "Unmanned" sounds empty. "Smart" sounds alive. |
| $3.75 espresso | Affordable coffee | A price IS the proof of affordability |
| 60 seconds | Fast / quick | A number IS the proof of speed |

### Never Use

| Term | Why |
|---|---|
| **Vending machine** | Triggers instant coffee association. Our kiosks are the opposite. |
| **Cheap** | We're accessible, not cheap. "Half the price of a coffee shop" — never "cheap coffee." |
| **Passive income** (alone) | Always pair with "semi-passive" or add context. The business needs servicing. Don't oversell ease. |
| **Guaranteed** / "you will earn" | Always conditional: "At 25 cups/day, your projected monthly profit is..." |
| **Disrupting** / "disruptive" | Silicon Valley cliche. We're building, not disrupting. |
| **Revolutionary** / "game-changer" | Show, don't declare. Let the numbers speak. |
| **Franchise** | Legally and strategically different. We sell licenses. |
| **Best coffee** | We say "barista-quality" or "rated 5/10 by experts, 9/10 by customers, and climbing." Honest > superlative. |
| **AI-powered** (in B2C) | Consumers don't care. Use "smart." Reserve "AI-Native" for investor/press contexts. |

### Numbers Rule

Numbers are our most persuasive tool. Use them aggressively.

| Weak | Strong |
|---|---|
| "High margins" | "300% markup. $1 in, $4 out." |
| "Quick payback" | "Payback in 10-18 months." |
| "Fast service" | "Bean-to-cup in 60 seconds." |
| "Large market" | "$56 billion out-of-home coffee market." |
| "Many partners" | "90+ licenses sold. 10+ states." |
| "Affordable" | "$3.75 for a real espresso." |
| "Growing fast" | "From 0 to 40 kiosks. 70 more installing this month." |

**Exception:** Don't use numbers when they weaken the story. "Team of 11" is fine internally but doesn't belong in a hero section.

## 4. Tone by Context

The voice stays the same. The tone shifts.

### Website — Homepage

**Tone:** Confident, visual, aspirational. Show the lifestyle, not just the product.

```
DO:
"Bean-to-cup espresso. 60 seconds. $3.75. No barista needed."

DON'T:
"Welcome to Manna Coffee, a leading provider of automated
self-service coffee solutions for the American market."
```

### Website — Product Pages

**Tone:** Specific, comparative, honest. Specs matter here.

```
DO:
"Nano Krea Touch — $13,000
UL/NSF certified. 8" touchscreen. Hot drinks only.
Serves 25-40 cups before refill. Daily servicing.
Best for: offices, gyms, waiting rooms."

DON'T:
"Our state-of-the-art Nano model offers an exceptional
coffee experience with cutting-edge technology."
```

### Website — For Operators (B2B)

**Tone:** Peer-to-peer. Business case. Respect their expertise.

```
DO:
"Your snack machines earn $0.40 per unit.
Our coffee kiosks earn $2.50 per cup. Same footprint.
Add bean-to-cup to your existing routes."

DON'T:
"Are you a vending operator looking for new revenue streams?
Discover how Manna Coffee can help grow your business!"
```

### Website — For Entrepreneurs

**Tone:** Mentor. Honest about effort, clear about reward.

```
DO:
"20 kiosks. 2 employees. Under 1 hour a day to manage.
Projected income: $400,000/year at 40 cups per kiosk.
It's a real business — not a get-rich-quick scheme."

DON'T:
"Start your dream business today with zero experience needed!
Earn passive income while you sleep!"
```

### Email — Lead Nurture

**Tone:** Personal, direct, value-first. Like a message from a person, not a brand.

```
DO:
Subject: "What 25 cups a day looks like"
"Hi [Name],
Quick math. 25 cups × $2.50 profit = $62.50/day.
That's $1,875/month from one kiosk. Before your second one.
Here's how our partners in [State] are hitting these numbers →"

DON'T:
Subject: "Exciting Business Opportunity Awaits!"
"Dear Valued Prospect,
Thank you for your interest in Manna Coffee. We are excited to
share information about our revolutionary coffee kiosk business..."
```

### Email — Reactivation

**Tone:** News-first. Short. One clear CTA.

```
DO:
Subject: "5,000 new locations just opened up"
"[Name], quick update.
We signed agreements with a major metro transit system (600 stations)
and a national property manager (4,500 buildings).
Send us your zip code — we'll check what's available near you.
[Reply with your zip code →]"

DON'T:
Subject: "We Miss You! Come Back to Manna Coffee"
"It's been a while since we heard from you..."
```

### Social Media

**Tone:** Behind-the-scenes. Real stories. Unpolished is fine.

```
DO:
"Our first kiosk was in an Armenian money transfer office
on Brighton Beach. The FBI raided them 3 months later
(nothing to do with us). Nikita had to rescue the kiosk at midnight.
That's startup life. #CoffeeLoveRobots"

DON'T:
"Exciting milestone! 🎉 We're thrilled to announce that
Manna Coffee is expanding to new locations! Stay tuned
for more updates! #Innovation #Coffee #Entrepreneurship"
```

### Investor / Press

**Tone:** Visionary, data-backed. Specific about market and traction.

```
DO:
"$56B out-of-home coffee market. 30,000 instant vending machines.
Near-zero bean-to-cup self-service.
90+ licenses sold, 40 kiosks operational, 70 more this month.
First AI-Native coffee company."

DON'T:
"Manna Coffee is poised to revolutionize the $110B coffee industry
with our proprietary AI-driven platform..."
```

## 5. Formatting Rules

### Headlines

- **Short.** 3-8 words ideal. Never a full sentence.
- **Concrete.** A number, a claim, a contrast.
- **No buzzwords.** If the headline works for any company, it's too generic.

| Good | Bad |
|---|---|
| "$3.75 espresso. No barista." | "The Future of Coffee Is Here" |
| "Bean-to-cup in 60 seconds" | "Fast, Fresh, Affordable Coffee" |
| "Your kiosk. Your profit." | "Empowering Entrepreneurs Everywhere" |
| "Coffee, Love & Robots" | "Innovation Meets Passion" |

### Body Copy

- **One idea per paragraph.** Max 3 sentences per block.
- **Front-load the point.** First sentence = the claim. Rest = the proof.
- **Use fragments.** "No plumbing. No barista. No franchise fee." is better than full sentences.
- **Active voice always.** "The kiosk brews espresso" — not "espresso is brewed by the kiosk."

### CTAs (Calls to Action)

- **Specific, not generic.** "Calculate your ROI" > "Learn More"
- **Verb-first.** "Get the business plan" / "See the kiosks" / "Send your zip code"
- **One primary CTA per section.** Never compete for attention.

| Good CTAs | Bad CTAs |
|---|---|
| "Calculate your ROI" | "Learn More" |
| "See all 6 models" | "Explore Our Products" |
| "Get the business plan (PDF)" | "Download Now" |
| "Send your zip code" | "Contact Us" |
| "Book a 15-min call" | "Get In Touch" |
| "Watch a kiosk brew a latte" | "View Demo" |

## 6. The Love Test

Before publishing any piece of content, ask:

1. **Is this honest?** Would Sergey say this to a friend considering the business? If it needs a disclaimer, rewrite it.
2. **Is there a number?** If the claim can be quantified, quantify it. "High margins" fails. "$2.50 profit per cup" passes.
3. **Is the partner the hero?** Are we empowering them or selling to them? "Your kiosk earns..." not "We provide..."
4. **Would I read this?** If it sounds like marketing, rewrite it. If it sounds like a smart person explaining something interesting, ship it.
5. **Coffee, Love & Robots — is each one present?** The product (Coffee), the human element (Love), the technology (Robots). Best content touches all three.

## 7. Voice Don'ts — Real Examples

### Don't: Corporate Distance

```
BAD:  "Manna Coffee is committed to providing best-in-class
       automated retail solutions for the self-service beverage industry."

GOOD: "We make smart kiosks that brew real espresso.
       You plug them in, people buy coffee, you make money."
```

### Don't: Hype Without Substance

```
BAD:  "Join the coffee revolution! Unlimited earning potential
       with our game-changing technology!"

GOOD: "At 25 cups a day, one kiosk nets you $800-1,200/month.
       Most partners start with 2-3 and scale from there."
```

### Don't: Emoji Overload

```
BAD:  "Great news! 🎉☕🤖 We just launched our NEW kiosk model!
       Check it out! 👇💰🔥"

GOOD: "New model: Pico GL-24. Smallest footprint, lowest entry price.
       $9,900. Same bean-to-cup quality. Details →"
```

### Don't: Fear-Based Selling

```
BAD:  "Don't miss out on this once-in-a-lifetime opportunity!
       Limited spots available! Act NOW before it's too late!"

GOOD: "We're adding 70 kiosks this month. If you've been thinking
       about it, now's a good time to talk numbers."
```

### Don't: Shifting Responsibility

```
BAD:  "We guarantee you'll find the perfect location!"

GOOD: "We train you on location scouting, give you our pitch deck
       for landlords, and share our location bank. You do the legwork.
       Best locations come from partners who know their neighborhood."
```
